---
title: Break-glass recovery
description: Design emergency recovery that requires multiple participants and ends with rotation.
---

A break-glass secret is used rarely and usually grants elevated access. The procedure should be short enough to run during an incident and strict enough to prevent casual use.

## Example custody model

A platform team needs emergency access to a cloud account if normal identity systems fail. They choose <span class="token">k=3, n=5</span>.

Suggested custody:

- Share 1: security lead.
- Share 2: operations lead.
- Share 3: engineering manager.
- Share 4: offline company safe.
- Share 5: executive sponsor or compliance custodian.

Three independent participants are required. One unavailable person does not block recovery, and one compromised holder cannot recover alone.

## Setup procedure

<Steps>
1. Generate or export the credential in a controlled environment.
2. Split it with the chosen threshold, encoding, and passphrase policy.
3. Give each custodian only their own share.
4. Record custody, holder contacts, threshold, and encoding.
5. Run a synthetic drill with the same process.
6. Destroy temporary files that contain the credential or all shares together.
</Steps>

Example command:

```bash
safeparts split \
  -k 3 \
  -n 5 \
  -e mnemo-words \
  --passphrase-file passphrase.txt \
  --in cloud-admin-recovery.txt \
  --out shares.txt
```

## Recovery procedure

<Steps>
1. Open the incident or emergency change record.
2. Confirm approval according to policy.
3. Collect at least <var>k</var> shares in the recovery environment.
4. Combine the shares and recover the credential.
5. Use the credential only for the approved task.
6. Rotate or disable the recovered credential.
7. Create a new Safeparts split for the replacement credential.
</Steps>

Example command:

```bash
safeparts combine \
  --passphrase-file passphrase.txt \
  --in selected-shares.txt \
  --out recovered-cloud-admin.txt
```

:::danger
After break-glass recovery, assume the gathered shares were exposed. Rotate the underlying credential and issue a fresh split.
:::

## Audit record

Record:

- who approved recovery;
- which custodians participated;
- when recovery started and ended;
- which credential or system was recovered;
- what rotation happened afterward.

Do not record share text, passphrases, screenshots of shares, or recovered credentials.

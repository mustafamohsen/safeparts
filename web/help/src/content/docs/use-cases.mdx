A good Safeparts plan is mostly logistics. The math decides how many shares are needed. Your plan decides who can find them, who can approve recovery, and what happens when someone is unavailable.

:::tip
Useful starting points: <span class="token">k=2, n=3</span> for personal recovery and <span class="token">k=3, n=5</span> for small teams.
:::

## Common plans

## Personal recovery: <span class="token">k=2, n=3</span>

Use this when the main risk is losing access, not managing a large approval process.

Suggested distribution:

- Share 1: a place you can reach quickly, separate from the original secret.
- Share 2: sealed offline copy on paper or metal.
- Share 3: off-site fallback with a trusted person or secure location.

<Steps>
1. Run a synthetic drill with the same threshold.
2. Split the real recovery secret.
3. Store each share in its assigned place.
4. Recover once with two shares if your policy allows it.
5. Record where each share lives without recording the share text.
</Steps>

## Team recovery: <span class="token">k=3, n=5</span>

Use this when recovery should require cooperation and still survive one or two unavailable people.

Suggested custody:

- Operations lead.
- Security lead.
- Engineering lead.
- Executive sponsor or compliance custodian.
- Offline company vault.

The runbook should name the approver, the recovery location, the participating roles, and the rotation step after recovery.

## Break-glass recovery

Break-glass secrets are high-impact and rarely used. Keep the process short, auditable, and practiced.

Required choices:

- Who can declare a break-glass event.
- Which custodians must participate.
- Where recovery happens.
- How the recovered credential is used.
- What gets rotated afterward.

:::danger
After a break-glass recovery, assume the gathered shares and recovered credential were exposed. Rotate the underlying credential and create a new split.
:::

## Holder selection

Choose holders and locations with independent failure modes.

<CardGrid>
	<Card title="Avoid co-location" icon="warning">
		Two shares in the same safe, vault item, ticket system, or cloud account can fail as one share.
	</Card>
	<Card title="Plan for absence" icon="setting">
		Recovery should still work during travel, illness, turnover, or an outage.
	</Card>
	<Card title="Document custody" icon="document">
		Record holder names or roles, contact paths, encoding, threshold, and drill schedule. Do not record the shares.
	</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Drill schedule

Run drills with synthetic data first. For production procedures, schedule a periodic custody check and a recovery drill that follows the same approval path without exposing live credentials.

A drill should confirm that holders can find their shares, operators know the commands or app workflow, and the team knows when to rotate secrets.
